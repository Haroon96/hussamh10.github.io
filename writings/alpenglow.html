Alpenglow

The mountains glow in the distance, lit by the sun and desires. They glow from light that almost slipped past our Earth. The light paints the ridges red, a color of where you are not.
For years I’ve been moved by the color of the early morning peaks: alpenglow. I've desired to get up close and see the fiery glow from within. So, one night we pitch a tent, wake in the dark, and drive through morning dew toward the foothills. The glow is still there. Yet as I get closer to the summit, closer to my desires, the red is fading.
The glow was a trick of my eyes, only seen when what you want is afar. Up close, the glow gives way to granite and wind, the desire gives way to a longing for another. You look back to your room, coddling in the rising sun. Glowing fiery red with the same light.
